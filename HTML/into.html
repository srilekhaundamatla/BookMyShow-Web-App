WHY WATCH INTO THE WOODS?

ONCE UPON A TIME

"Into the Woods" is a musical film released in 2014, based on the popular Broadway musical of the same name by Stephen Sondheim and James Lapine. The film weaves together several well-known fairy tales, including Little Red Riding Hood, Cinderella, and Jack and the Beanstalk, and follows the characters as they journey into the woods in search of their wishes and dreams. Overall, "Into the Woods" is a magical and enchanting musical that combines humor, drama, and fantasy elements to create a timeless story about the power of love, family, and the importance of making choices that have lasting consequences.

WHAT'S THE STORY?

The main story revolves around a Baker and his wife, who are unable to have children due to a curse placed on their family by a wicked witch. In order to break the curse, they must collect several items from the other characters in the woods, including a red cape, a golden slipper, and a white cow. As they journey through the woods, the characters encounter challenges and obstacles, including a menacing giant who threatens to destroy the kingdom. They must work together and learn important lessons about responsibility, sacrifice, and the consequences of their actions in order to achieve their goals and overcome the dangers that lie ahead.The film features an all-star cast, including Meryl Streep as the Witch, Emily Blunt as the Baker's Wife, James Corden as the Baker, Anna Kendrick as Cinderella, and Chris Pine as Prince Charming. The music is composed by Stephen Sondheim and includes popular songs such as "No One Is Alone" and "Children Will Listen."

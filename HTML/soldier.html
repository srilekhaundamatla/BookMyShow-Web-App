WHAT'S THE STORY?

"A Soldier's Play" is a play written by Charles Fuller that explores themes of racism, prejudice, and identity in the context of the military during World War II. The story is set in Louisiana in 1944 and follows an African-American army sergeant named Vernon Waters, who is murdered on a Louisiana Army base. The investigation into his death is led by Captain Richard Davenport, an African-American lawyer who must navigate the racial tensions and divisions within the army to uncover the truth. Through a series of flashbacks and interviews with soldiers, Davenport uncovers the events and underlying prejudices that led to Waters' death. The play examines issues of racism, prejudice, and identity, and offers a complex portrait of the African-American experience in the military during World War II. "A Soldier's Play" won the Pulitzer Prize for Drama in 1982 and has been adapted into a film titled "A Soldier's Story."

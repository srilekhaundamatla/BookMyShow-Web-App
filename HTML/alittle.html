WHY WATCH A LITTLE NIGHT MUSIC ?

ONCE UPON A TIME

It is a show that explores complex and timeless themes of love, sex, desire, and regret. The characters are all flawed and complex, and their relationships are portrayed with nuance and sensitivity. As such, the show offers a thought-provoking and emotionally engaging experience that is sure to resonate with audiences. "A Little Night Music" is a classic of musical theater that has been praised by critics and audiences alike for decades. The show has been revived numerous times and has won multiple Tony Awards, cementing its place as a beloved and enduring part of the musical theater canon.

WHAT'S THE STORY?

"A Little Night Music" is a musical with music and lyrics by Stephen Sondheim and a book by Hugh Wheeler. The show is based on the 1955 Ingmar Bergman film "Smiles of a Summer Night" and tells the story of the romantic entanglements of a group of people over the course of a weekend in the Swedish countryside. The musical is known for its lush and melodic score, which includes the famous song "Send in the Clowns," and for its witty and sophisticated book. The show explores themes of love, sex, jealousy, and regret, and features a cast of complex and memorable characters.

WHY WATCH HAIRSPRAY?

ONCE UPON A TIME

The movie "Hairspray" is a musical comedy based on the 2002 Broadway musical of the same name, which was in turn based on the 1988 film by John Waters. Set in Baltimore in the 1960s. The film is filled with catchy musical numbers and lively dance sequences, as well as humor and heart. Ultimately, "Hairspray" is a story about the power of inclusivity, acceptance, and standing up for what is right, and it offers a fun and inspiring message that is sure to resonate with audiences of all ages.

WHAT'S THE STORY?

The movie "Hairspray" ells the story of Tracy Turnblad, a teenage girl who dreams of dancing on "The Corny Collins Show," a popular local TV dance program. Despite facing discrimination and ridicule for her size and unconventional appearance, Tracy is determined to achieve her dream, and she begins to attend auditions for the show. Along the way, she makes new friends, including a group of African American students who are fighting for integration and equal rights. As Tracy's star rises on the show, she becomes increasingly involved in the civil rights movement and begins to use her newfound platform to advocate for change. However, she faces opposition from the show's producer, Velma Von Tussle, and her scheming daughter, Amber, who are determined to maintain the status quo and keep the show segregated.

WHY WATCH SUNDAY IN THE PARK WITH GEROGE ?

ONCE UPON A TIME

"Sunday in the Park with George" is a musical that tells the story of the painter Georges Seurat and his creation of the famous painting "A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte." The musical explores themes such as art, love, relationships, and the creative process.The show also offers a commentary on the nature of art and the artist's struggle to balance their personal and creative lives, which can be relatable to anyone who has ever pursued a passion or creative endeavor. Ultimately, "Sunday in the Park with George" is a poignant and thought-provoking piece of theater that offers insight into the human experience and the power of art to inspire and transform.

WHAT'S THE STORY?

Sunday in the Park with George is a musical with music and lyrics by Stephen Sondheim and book by James Lapine. It was inspired by the French pointillist painter Georges Seurat's painting A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte. The plot revolves around George, a fictionalized version of Seurat, who immerses himself deeply in painting his masterpiece, and his great-grandson (also named George), a conflicted and cynical contemporary artist. The Broadway production opened in 1984.
